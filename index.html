FAQs

What is Covid- 19?

COVID-19 is a disease by a virus calles SARS-CoV-2. Most people with COVID-19 have mild symptoms, but some people can become
severely ill. Although most people with COVID-19 get better within weeks of illness, some people experience post-COVID conditions. Post-
COVID conditions are a wide range of new, returning, or ongoing health problems people can experience more than four weeks after first
being infected with the virus that cause COVID-19. Older people and those who have certain underlying medical conditions are more likely to
get severly ill from COVID-19. Vaccines against COVID-19 are safe and effective.

Should I use soap and water or hand sanitizer to protect myself?

Handwashing is one of the best ways to protect yourself and your family from getting sick. Wash your hand often with soap and water for at
least 20 seconds, especially after blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezinf; going to bathroom; and before eating or preparing food. If
soap water are not readily available, use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol/

What are symptoms and complications with COVID-19 can cause?

People with Covid-19 have reported a wide range of symptoms - from mild symptoms to severe illness. Symptoms may appear 2-14 days
after exposure to the virus. If you have fever, cough, or other symptoms, you might have COVID-19.
